How a store reaches plateau Typical post-opening trajectory
Weeks 1–4
Sharp decline
20–35% drop from opening week as novelty fades
Weeks 5–7
Deceleration
Drop slows significantly — loyal base forming
Weeks 8–10
Leveling out
Day-to-day variance but no clear downward trend
Weeks 11–12+
True plateau
Stable daily average — loyal repeat base established
Rule of thumb: By week 12, you know what you have. A store that hasn't stabilized by then is a performance flag, not normal settling.
Exception: A small number of stores show continued slow decline past week 12 — Sherwood and Granite Falls follow this pattern, suggesting the market hasn't built a loyal repeat customer base.

Planning implications How to use plateau data operationally
Royalty projections
Use 67% of the first full week daily average as the long-term run rate for royalty modeling. A store opening at $4,000/day should be underwritten at ~$2,680/day ongoing.
Franchisee expectations
Set expectations pre-opening that week 1 volume is not the baseline. The 20–35% decline in weeks 1–4 is normal and expected — franchisees who aren't prepared for this become unnecessarily alarmed.
Intervention threshold
Flag any store whose Wk 1→4 change exceeds −35% for proactive outreach. Historical data shows these stores average 54% plateau retention — meaningful lost royalty revenue vs the 67% baseline.
Underperformance definition
A plateau below 55% retention is a material underperformance signal requiring a formal review. 7 stores across 2024–2025 have hit this threshold — Augusta, Richmond, Carthage, Sherwood, Brookings, Poplar Bluff, and Granite Falls.
